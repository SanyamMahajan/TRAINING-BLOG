Task Execution & Challenges

I focused heavily on problem-solving today, tackling four well-known DSA problems that are commonly asked in interviews:

  • ➕ 3Sum Problem
  • ➕➕ 4Sum Problem
  • 📈 Maximum Subarray (Kadane's Algorithm)
  • 🔢 Maximum Consecutive Ones

It took quite a bit of time and mental effort to crack 3Sum and 4Sum as they involve two-pointer strategies, nested loops, and careful duplicate handling.

Challenges faced:

  • ⚠️ Implementing the optimal two-pointer approach for 3Sum required managing index boundaries very precisely.
  • 🧠 4Sum was an extension of 3Sum and brought in higher time complexity — needed to think about pruning and avoiding duplicates effectively.
  • 📊 While Kadane’s Algorithm was relatively simpler, it was great revision and a reminder of the elegance in dynamic programming.
  • ⏱️ I spent most of my day debugging edge cases and testing inputs before reaching the correct final answers.
